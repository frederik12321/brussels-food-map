How We Rank Restaurants

A transparent look at the Brussels Score algorithm

TL;DR

We don't just show the highest-rated restaurants. We look for places that do better than expected for their type — taking into account cuisine, location, and how many reviews they have.

The Problem with Star Ratings

Google ratings are useful but not perfect. A 4.5-star restaurant near Grand Place isn't the same as a 4.5-star spot in a quieter neighbourhood. Busy tourist areas get more reviews from visitors, while neighbourhood spots get reviews from regulars.

Our algorithm tries to answer: "How good is this restaurant compared to similar places?"

The Brussels Score

Every restaurant gets a score from 0-100 based on multiple factors. Here's how each component works:

Base Quality 35% weight

The Google rating normalized to our scale. A 4.8-star restaurant scores higher than a 4.2-star one. This ensures quality still matters.

Outperforms Peers +20% max

We calculate what rating a restaurant "should" have based on its cuisine, price, and location. If it scores higher than expected, that's a good sign.

Example:
A Vietnamese restaurant in Ixelles has 4.6 stars. Similar restaurants in the area average 4.2. That's +0.4 stars above expected — it's doing something well.
Goldilocks Zone +8% max

Restaurants with 50-500 reviews hit a sweet spot: enough reviews to be trustworthy, but not so many that it's only because they're in a busy tourist area.

The zones:
  • 50-200 reviews: Full bonus (established, not over-hyped)
  • 200-500 reviews: Good bonus (popular, good reputation)
  • 500-1000 reviews: Small bonus (well-known)
  • 1000+ reviews: No bonus (likely high-traffic location)
Horseshoe Bonus +4% max

We give a small bonus to restaurants at both ends of the hours spectrum.

The Lark (Limited Hours):
Closes mid-afternoon (service coupé), or open less than 30h/week. Think serious bistros, bakeries with fresh prep.
The Owl (Late Night):
Open past 1:00 AM regularly. Think late-night pittas, frituurs, neighbourhood bars.
The Middle (No bonus):
Standard hours like 11:00-22:00 every day. Most common, no extra points.
Guide Recognition +8% max

Michelin stars, Bib Gourmand, and Gault&Millau recognition. These guides send trained inspectors who evaluate restaurants anonymously.

Reddit Mentions +5% max

Restaurants that people on r/brussels recommend when someone asks "where should I eat?" We count how often each place gets mentioned.

Diaspora Bonus +7% max

A Moroccan restaurant in Molenbeek or a Congolese restaurant in Matongé gets a bonus — they're in the heart of their community. Extra points on known food streets like Chaussée de Haecht (Turkish) or Rue de Brabant (Maghreb).

How it works:
Only the matching cuisine gets the bonus. An Italian restaurant on Chaussée de Haecht gets nothing — only Turkish restaurants do.

Penalties

Some factors reduce a restaurant's score:

Tourist Area Penalty -8% max

Restaurants near Grand Place, Manneken Pis, and other tourist hotspots lose some points. These areas have lots of foot traffic, and ratings there tend to be less reliable.

Low Review Penalty -20% max

A 4.9-star rating with only 8 reviews doesn't mean much — it could just be friends and family. The fewer reviews, the bigger the penalty, especially for suspiciously high ratings.

Penalty scale:
  • <10 reviews + high rating: -15% to -20%
  • 10-20 reviews + high rating: -8% to -12%
  • 20-50 reviews: -3% to -6%
  • 50-200 reviews with perfect 5.0: -1% to -2%

What "Above Expected" Means

When you see "+0.3 stars above expected", here's what it means:

  1. We look at similar restaurants (same cuisine, price range, area)
  2. We calculate what rating this restaurant "should" have based on those factors
  3. If the actual rating is higher, that's a positive sign
  4. The difference (+0.3) shows how much better it is than expected

Restaurants that score above expected are doing something right compared to similar places.

Data Sources

  • Restaurant data: Google Maps (ratings, reviews, hours, location)
  • Guide recognition: Michelin Guide Belgium, Gault&Millau
  • Community picks: r/brussels recommendation threads
  • Food safety: AFSCA/FAVV smiley database

Limitations

No ranking is perfect. Here's where ours falls short:

  • New restaurants with few reviews will score lower (we can't trust the rating yet)
  • Some places don't fit neatly into one cuisine category
  • We don't have complete price data for all restaurants
  • Google reviews have their own biases, and we inherit those
  • Opening hours might be outdated for some places
